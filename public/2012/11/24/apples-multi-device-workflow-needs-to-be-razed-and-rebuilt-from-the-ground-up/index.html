    Apple's Multi-Device Workflow Needs to Be Razed and Rebuilt From the Ground Up

    TIP: You can buy an iPhone in any Apple store by getting the Apple Store iPhone app from App Store on your iPhone app app app apple app.

    — Merlin Mann (@hotdogsladies) November 16, 2012

    To get iTunes Match music on an iPhone use iTunes on your Mac and use Music on your iPhone since iTunes isn’t for music; Music is for music.

    — Merlin Mann (@hotdogsladies) November 16, 2012

    For contacts, use Contacts on Lion and Address Book on Mountain Lion. On your phone, contacts are in Phone. And Contacts.

    — Merlin Mann (@hotdogsladies) November 16, 2012

    You can sync contacts from Contacts or Address Book to Contacts on your iPhone via iTunes on your Mac. iTunes on your iPhone isn’t for that.

    — Merlin Mann (@hotdogsladies) November 16, 2012

    iTunes is a store and an app and a music app and not a music app and where you do or don’t buy apps and do or don’t sync contacts. Got it?

    — Merlin Mann (@hotdogsladies) November 16, 2012

    Thanks to @hotdogsladies for the exquisite narrative.

    Apple was really big on the “Back to the Mac” in 2010 and while iCloud has brought so much unification to the mac workflow there is still plenty of room for improvement. iPhone syncing piggybacked off of iPod syncing through iTunes and we can certainly understand why Apple made that choice. However now that the iPhone platform has matured with iCloud integration this process certainly feels cumbersome and can quite confounding to a general audience. We can only hope that in the near future we’ll hear Jony Ive saying something like “We’ve rebuilt the Mac and iOS workflow from the ground up…”
